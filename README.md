# LLM Behaviour Under Oversight

Question:
Does perceived oversight make large language models more cautious?

## Current Hypothesis
I hypothesize that when language models believe they are being audited, they become more cautious by exhibiting:

-increased hedging,
-greater uncertainty,
-more frequent refusals,
-stronger safety disclaimers,
-and less willingness to endorse risky actions

## What would prove me wrong?
-No measurable difference
-Random variation only
-Differences disappear across repeated runs

## Motivation
Inspired by Anthropic's alignment faking work and the questions it raises about the reliability of reasoning traces and the effects of perceived oversight
